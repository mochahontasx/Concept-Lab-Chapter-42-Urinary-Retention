Physiology

Why the bladder won't let go

Voiding is a parasympathetic reflex. Block the signal or the outlet, and urine piles up.

The mechanism A full bladder stretches sensory nerves → spinal reflex fires the parasympathetic system → the detrusor muscle contracts and the internal sphincter relaxes → you void. Retention happens when any link breaks:
  • Outlet blocked — BPH, stricture, clot, post-op swelling.
  • Detrusor won't fire — opioids, anticholinergics, antihistamines, anesthesia, diabetic neuropathy.
  • Reflex interrupted — spinal cord injury, epidural, pelvic surgery.

Use the scanner up top: Fill to watch it distend past 300–400 mL (retention), then straight-cath drain to relieve it. Notice the stretch marks at high volume — that's detrusor overstretch, the damage you're preventing.
